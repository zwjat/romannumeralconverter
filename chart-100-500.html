How to Read Roman Numerals 100 - 500

Roman numerals from 500 to 1000 follow specific patterns. Here's a quick guide to understanding them:

Key Symbols:

  • D = 500
  • C = 100
  • L = 50
  • X = 10
  • V = 5
  • I = 1

Rules for Reading Roman Numerals:

  • When a smaller numeral appears after a larger one, it is added
  • When a smaller numeral appears before a larger one, it is subtracted
  • Only I, X, and C can be used as subtractive numerals
  • A numeral cannot be repeated more than three times in a row

Examples:

  • 600 = DC (500 + 100)
  • 700 = DCC (500 + 100 + 100)
  • 900 = CM (1000 - 100)
  • 950 = CML (1000 - 100 + 50)
